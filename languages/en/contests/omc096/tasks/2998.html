As shown in the diagram below, a rectangular space is divided into 2×52\times52×5 sections by boundary lines (dashed lines), and neighboring sections can freely move between them due to the boundary lines. Here, we will choose 444 out of the 131313 boundary lines between sections to build walls. Once a wall is built on a boundary line, it becomes impossible to move between the sections separated by that line. How many ways can we choose 444 boundary lines that satisfy the following condition?

  • For any two distinct sections, it must be possible to move from one to the other.

Note that rotations and reflections that make sections identical are also counted as distinct.
