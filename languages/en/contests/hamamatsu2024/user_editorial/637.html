The Ferrari method is effective when solving a quartic equation without a cubic term.
( * Although it is not relevant this time, even if there is a cubic term, you can transform it into a quartic polynomial without a cubic term by using y=x+ky=x+ky=x+k with an appropriate constant kkk.)

If the right side of the third line is a square of a linear polynomial, then this quartic polynomial can be factored into the product of two quadratic polynomials.
