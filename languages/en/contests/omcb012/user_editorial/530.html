This section introduces a method using (sum) = (average) × (count).
It is a very useful technique for problems that have symmetry and where the average can be easily calculated.

If abcde‾\overline{abcde}abcde is a good number, then the number obtained by replacing each digit with 10−a,10−b,⋯10-a, 10-b, \cdots10−a,10−b,⋯ is also a good number (for example, 262262622626226 and 848848488484884). For such pairs of good numbers, their average will always be 555555555555555. Therefore, the average of good numbers is also 555555555555555.
Now, we just need to find the count of good numbers. There are 9C2{}_{9}\mathrm{C}_{2}9​C2​ ways to choose the two types of numbers used in good numbers, and for each case, we can create 303030 different numbers.
Thus, the value we seek is 55555×(9C2×30)=5999940055555×({}_{9}\mathrm{C}_{2}×30)=\mathbf{59999400}55555×(9​C2​×30)=59999400.
