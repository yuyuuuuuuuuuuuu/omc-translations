In general, for a triangle ABCABCABC, let the circumcenter be OOO, the circumradius be RRR, and the inradius be rrr. In this case, if we denote the signed lengths of the perpendiculars dropped from OOO to BC,CA,ABBC, CA, ABBC,CA,AB as a,b,ca, b, ca,b,c, then the equation a+b+c=R+ra+b+c=R+ra+b+c=R+r holds. Here, the signed length of the perpendicular is defined to be negative only when the entire perpendicular is outside triangle ABCABCABC.
(This property is called Carnot's theorem, and for the case when triangle ABCABCABC is an acute triangle, an elegant proof can be found in Chen's article at 'https://mathlog.info/articles/2581'.)
