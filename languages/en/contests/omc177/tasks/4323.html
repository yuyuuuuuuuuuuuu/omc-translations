There are 313131 squares arranged in a row, numbered from left to right as 0,1,2,…,300,1,2,\dots,300,1,2,…,30. Initially, there is one piece on square 000, and we perform exactly 303030 operations on this piece. On the iii-th operation (i=1,…,30i=1,\ldots,30i=1,…,30), one of the following two actions can be taken:

  1. If the piece is currently on square xxx, move the piece to square x+1x+1x+1.
  2. If the piece was on square yyy just before the operation before the i−1i-1i−1-th one, move the piece to square yyy.

However, note that the second action cannot be chosen during the first operation.
There are a total of 2292^{29}229 ways to choose the operations (the movements of the piece are distinguished among the two types of operations even if they coincide), but how many ways are there to choose the operations such that ultimately the piece lands on square 242424?
