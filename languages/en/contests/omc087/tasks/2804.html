There are two universities, and 10 students from each will participate in a party. Initially, the participants are arranged into 5 groups of 4 people each, with 2 students from each university in every group.
Now, to deepen the participants' friendship, we decided to rearrange the groups. At this time, we must rearrange the groups so that there are still 2 students from each university in each group, and the following conditions must be satisfied:

  • There are no pairs of students who were in the same group before and after the rearrangement (regardless of whether the two students are from the same university).

When the initial arrangement is fixed, how many ways are there to rearrange the groups while satisfying these conditions?
Note that the groups themselves are indistinguishable (i.e., we only consider the division of students).
