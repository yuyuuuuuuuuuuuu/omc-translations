OMC-kun came to the fruit shop to purchase apples. The shop had enough of each of the 4 varieties of apples: Fuji, Kougyoku, Ohrin, and Shinano Sweet in packs of "[Variety Name] 2j2^j2j pieces" for each j=0,1,2,…,13j=0,1,2,…,13j=0,1,2,…,13. OMC-kun wants to combine these packs to purchase exactly 111111111111111 apples (it is okay not to buy some varieties). Furthermore, he wants to purchase an even total number of 2-piece packs (considering all varieties together).
In this case, we define a good way to buy when the total number of packs purchased (for all varieties and counts combined) is even, and a bad way to buy when the total is odd.
Please find the absolute difference in the number of combinations for good and bad ways to buy. Note that identical varieties and identical counts in packs are not distinguished, and the order of selecting packs does not matter. Also, 000 is considered even.

Examples of Buying Methods

  • Fuji 2132^{13}213 pieces, Fuji 2112^{11}211 pieces, Fuji 292^929 pieces
  • Kougyoku 282^828 pieces, Kougyoku 262^626 pieces, Fuji 232^323 pieces
  • Fuji 232^323 pieces, Kougyoku 232^323 pieces, Ohrin 222^222 pieces
  • Ohrin 222^222 pieces, Ohrin 212^121 pieces, Fuji 212^121 pieces
  • Kougyoku 202^020 pieces, Ohrin 202^020 pieces, Shinano Sweet 202^020 pieces

When purchasing a total of 151515 packs, the total comes to 111111111111111 pieces and there are 222 packs of 2-piece packs, which satisfies the conditions and results in an odd total number of packs making it a bad way to buy.
