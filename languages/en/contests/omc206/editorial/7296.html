Since \(7!=2^4\cdot3^2\cdot5\cdot7\), the divisors of \(7!\) can be expressed in the form \(2^a\cdot3^b\cdot5^c\cdot7^d\).
The product of two elements is a perfect square if and only if the parity of the exponents of each prime factor \(2, 3, 5, 7\) matches. Therefore, when dividing the divisors of \(7!\) into \(16\ (=2^4)\) sets \(A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_{16}\) based on the combinations of the parity of the powers of the prime factors \(2, 3, 5, 7\), it is possible to form a good set by selecting one element from several different sets, specifically giving \(N=16\).
The sum of the products of the elements of a good set with \(16\) elements is equal to the product of the sums of the elements of each of \(A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_{16}\) (considering the expansion of the expression). Moreover, the sum of the elements of each of \(A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_{16}\) is obtained by selecting one element from each of the following four sets and multiplying.
