In a grid of 16×1616 \times 1616×16, we draw three thick lines vertically and horizontally, dividing it into 16 squares of 4×44 \times 44×4. When we refer to these 4×44 \times 44×4 squares as blocks, how many ways can we color 16 out of the 16216^2162 squares black such that exactly one square in each row, column, and block is colored black? Note that coloring methods that coincide through rotation or reflection are also distinguished.
