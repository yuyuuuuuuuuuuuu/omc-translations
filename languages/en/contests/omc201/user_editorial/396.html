"Why can we say that the edge of cube AAA is equal to the length of the diagonal of cube CCC?"
Hint: Imagine transferring lengths with A,B,CA, B, CA,B,C.

Explanation (tap to open) By drawing a diagram, we can see that the edge of AAA is equal to the diameter of sphere BBB.

Additionally, all vertices of CCC are on the surface of sphere BBB, and the vertex that is exactly opposite to a certain vertex of CCC (※) is also exactly opposite on the sphere's surface. The distance between these two vertices is, from the perspective of CCC, the length of the diagonal of CCC, and from the perspective of BBB, the length of the diameter of BBB. Therefore, these lengths are equal.

Thus, we have established that the edge of AAA is equal to the length of the diagonal of CCC.

(※) For example, if we label the vertices of CCC as EFGH−IJKLEFGH-IJKLEFGH−IJKL, then the "vertex opposite to EEE at CCC" refers to KKK.
