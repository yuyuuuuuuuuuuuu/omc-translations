We name the initial configuration of the 5 groups as A,B,C,D,EA,B,C,D,EA,B,C,D,E. At this point, we consider the following table:

{D,E}{C,E}{A,B}{A,B}{C,D}{B,C}{A,D}{D,E}{C,E}{A,B}\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|} \hline \{D,E\} & \{C,E\} & \{A,B\} & \{A,B\} & \{C,D\} \\ \hline \hline \{B,C\} & \{A,D\} & \{D,E\} & \{C,E\} & \{A,B\} \\ \hline \end{array}{D,E}{B,C}​{C,E}{A,D}​{A,B}{D,E}​{A,B}{C,E}​{C,D}{A,B}​​
Each column represents the composition of the groups after rearrangement, and the top and bottom correspond to each university. Therefore, the constraints on the arrangement of letters are:

  • Each column contains 4 different letters.
  • Each letter appears twice in total at the top and bottom respectively.

Furthermore, without losing generality, we consider that the only letter that does not appear in each column is either A,B,C,D,A,B,C,D,A,B,C,D, or EEE. We thus need to consider how many such tables exist. However, it's important to note that since students who belonged to the same group from the same university are not distinguished in the beginning, we need to multiply the final result by 2102^{10}210. Since determining the arrangement of the top 10 letters uniquely determines the rest, we reduce the problem to the following:

  • There are 5 boxes labeled 1,2,3,4,51,2,3,4,51,2,3,4,5, and 10 balls, each labeled A,A,B,B,C,C,D,D,E,EA,A,B,B,C,C,D,D,E,EA,A,B,B,C,C,D,D,E,E. What is the number of ways to place 2 balls in each box such that the following conditions are met?
    • The same box cannot hold 2 balls with the same label.
    • A ball labeled with AAA cannot be placed in box 111.
    • A ball labeled with BBB cannot be placed in box 222.
    • A ball labeled with CCC cannot be placed in box 333.
    • A ball labeled with DDD cannot be placed in box 444.
    • A ball labeled with EEE cannot be placed in box 555.

Here, we prepare vertices 1,2,3,4,51,2,3,4,51,2,3,4,5, and for example, if we place a ball labeled AAA in boxes iii and jjj, we draw an edge labeled AAA between vertices iii and jjj to construct an undirected graph with 5 vertices and 5 edges. In this case, the overall structure of the graph is:

  • One cycle of length 5.
  • One cycle of length 3, and one cycle of length 2 (i.e., one multiple edge).

For each case, the number of ways to draw edges is 12 and 10, respectively, and for a given drawing, the number of ways to assign labels is 13 and 6. This confirms that the total number of cases we seek is:

(12×13+10×6)×210=221184.(12\times 13+10\times 6)\times 2^{10}=\textbf{221184}.(12×13+10×6)×210=221184.
