Mr. TKG, who has memorized the multiplication table, wants to memorize the results of multiplying two-digit positive integers, which amounts to 99299^2992 entries. He prepared a 99×9999 \times 9999×99 grid (an extended multiplication table) such that the cell in the ithi^{th}ith row and jthj^{th}jth column corresponds to i×ji \times ji×j. Then, he decided that the cell corresponding to a×ba \times ba×b does not need to be memorized and colored it black if at least one of the following conditions for the two-digit positive integers aaa and bbb is satisfied:

  • a>ba > ba>b.
  • Both aaa and bbb are one-digit numbers.
  • At least one of aaa or bbb is equal to 111.
  • At least one of aaa or bbb is a multiple of 101010.

In this case, how many cells remain uncolored?
