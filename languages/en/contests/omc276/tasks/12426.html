There is a 131313 row by 131313 column grid where each cell is painted either white or black. We consider a board configuration where each of the 169169169 cells is painted either white or black. In this grid, you can perform one of the following two operations in a single move:

  • Select a specific row from the 131313 rows and invert the color of all the cells in that row. This means changing black cells to white and white cells to black.

  • Select a specific column from the 131313 columns and invert the color of all the cells in that column. This means changing black cells to white and white cells to black.

Now, how many initial configurations of the board can be made such that all cells can be made the same color by performing the above operations at most 111111 times?
Note that configurations where all cells are the same color are considered to have achieved the condition with 000 operations.
