We repeatedly perform the following operation on a 4×44\times 44×4 grid where no squares have coins placed on them:

  • Select a square that does not have a coin and where all adjacent squares (sharing an edge) also do not have coins, and place 1 coin in the selected square.

When it becomes impossible to place coins in all squares, please find the product of the maximum and minimum possible number of squares that have coins placed on them.
