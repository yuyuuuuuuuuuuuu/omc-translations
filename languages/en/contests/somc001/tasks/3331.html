How many ways can we fill each cell of a 3×33 \times 33×3 grid with integers from 111 to 999, each used exactly once, such that the following condition is met? Here, we do not distinguish between arrangements that can be obtained by rotation or reflection.

  • For every 2×22 \times 22×2 sub-grid, it must contain a cell that has a multiple of 444 written in it.

Note that a "sub-grid of 2×22\times 22×2" refers to the shared area of two adjacent rows and two adjacent columns, which consists of 444 cells.
