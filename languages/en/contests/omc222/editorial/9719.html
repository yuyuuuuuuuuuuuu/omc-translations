By using up to 2 pieces of ¥1! to ¥3! = 6 yen bills, it is possible to pay exact amounts from ¥1 to ¥18 in 1 yen increments. On the other hand, for \( n \geq 4 \), it can be shown inductively that

2⋅1!+2⋅2!+⋯+2⋅(n−1)!<n! 2\cdot1!+2\cdot2!+\cdots+2\cdot(n-1)! \lt n!2⋅1!+2⋅2!+⋯+2⋅(n−1)!<n!
This indicates that the amount made using the ¥n! bills, with all the bills used being fewer than ¥n!, becomes larger than the amounts made using two of each bill, leading to the conclusion that the total amounts corresponding to the number of ¥n! bills used are distinct. Therefore, for \( n \geq 4 \), the number of different amounts that can be made using bills up to ¥n! is \( 19 \cdot 3^{n-3}-1 \), and these can be uniquely represented by
