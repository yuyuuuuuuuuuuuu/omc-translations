Angle chasing alone can prove that MA=MRMA = MRMA=MR, so I will introduce it.

Let point NNN be the intersection of line ADADAD and circle ω\omegaω, which is not point AAA. In this case, point NNN is the midpoint of arc BCBCBC, and BC⊥MNBC \perp MNBC⊥MN.

Step 1: The four points D,M,N,QD, M, N, QD,M,N,Q are concyclic.
(Proof) Let point N′N'N′ be the intersection of line MNMNMN and circle ω\omegaω, which is not point NNN.
We have ∠FQN+∠ANN′=∠FQN+∠FNN′=90∘\angle{FQN} + \angle{ANN'} = \angle{FQN} + \angle{FNN'} = 90^{\circ}∠FQN+∠ANN′=∠FQN+∠FNN′=90∘ (the last equality follows from the fact that it is the sum of the inscribed angles subtended by arc FNFNFN and arc FN′FN'FN′).
Furthermore, since triangle △DMN\triangle DMN△DMN is a right triangle, it follows that ∠MQN=∠MDN\angle{MQN} = \angle{MDN}∠MQN=∠MDN.

Step 2: △ADM≡△RDM\triangle ADM \equiv \triangle RDM△ADM≡△RDM
(Proof) Let point Q′Q'Q′ be the intersection of line AMAMAM and circle ω\omegaω, which is not point AAA. Since points QQQ and Q′Q'Q′ are symmetric with respect to line MNMNMN, we have ∠NAQ=∠NAQ′\angle{NAQ} = \angle{NAQ'}∠NAQ=∠NAQ′. From this, we obtain ∠DAM=∠DAQ=∠DRQ=∠DRM\angle{DAM} = \angle{DAQ} = \angle{DRQ} = \angle{DRM}∠DAM=∠DAQ=∠DRQ=∠DRM.
Additionally, using the property of symmetry, we get ∠DMR=∠DMA\angle{DMR} = \angle{DMA}∠DMR=∠DMA.
Therefore, since one side length is equal (common side DMDMDM) and the corresponding two angles are equal, we have △ADM≡△RDM\triangle ADM \equiv \triangle RDM△ADM≡△RDM.
