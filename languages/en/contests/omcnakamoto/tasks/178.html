In a certain junior high school, a class of 40\displaystyle 4040 students conducted a survey. The survey asked students to answer whether they were "good at" or "bad at" each subject: Japanese, Math, Science, Social Studies, and English. From the results of this survey, the following information is known:

  • No one answered "bad at" for all subjects.
  • There were 18\displaystyle 1818 students who answered consistently for both Japanese and Social Studies, as well as for Math and Science.
  • There were 15\displaystyle 1515 students who answered consistently for both Math and English, as well as for Science and Social Studies.
  • There were 12\displaystyle 1212 students who answered consistently for both Science and Japanese, as well as for Social Studies and English.
  • There were 9\displaystyle 99 students who answered consistently for both Social Studies and Math, as well as for English and Japanese.
  • There were 6\displaystyle 66 students who answered consistently for both English and Science, as well as for Japanese and Math.

At this time, please find all possible numbers of students who answered "good at" for all subjects and provide their total sum as the answer.
