The line related to the median of a triangle and the isogonal conjugate (specifically, the line AOAOAO in triangle ABCABCABC) is called the symmedian, and various properties are known about it. This lemma can be considered a starting point for those properties, particularly the main one.

Lemma. Let Ω\OmegaΩ be the circumcircle of triangle ABCABCABC, and let XXX be the intersection point of the tangents to Ω\OmegaΩ at points BBB and CCC. Then, we have ∠BAX=∠CAM\angle BAX = \angle CAM∠BAX=∠CAM.

Proof. We need to show that if we take a point M′M'M′ on side BCBCBC such that ∠BAX=∠CAM′\angle BAX = \angle CAM'∠BAX=∠CAM′, then BM′=CM′BM' = CM'BM′=CM′. From the sine rule, we have

BM′=AM×sin⁡∠BAM′sin⁡B,  CM′=AM×sin⁡∠CAM′sin⁡CBM' = AM \times \dfrac{\sin\angle BAM'}{\sin B},\ \ CM' = AM \times \dfrac{\sin\angle CAM'}{\sin C}BM′=AM×sinBsin∠BAM′​,  CM′=AM×sinCsin∠CAM′​
On the other hand, from the tangential secant theorem, we have ∠ABX=180∘−∠C\angle ABX = 180^\circ - \angle C∠ABX=180∘−∠C, and again using the sine rule, we find
AX×sin⁡∠BAXsin⁡∠C=BO=CO=AX×sin⁡∠CAXsin⁡∠BAX \times \dfrac{\sin\angle BAX}{\sin\angle C} = BO = CO = AX \times \dfrac{\sin\angle CAX}{\sin\angle B}AX×sin∠Csin∠BAX​=BO=CO=AX×sin∠Bsin∠CAX​
From these, considering how point M′M'M′ is taken, we conclude that BM′=CM′BM' = CM'BM′=CM′ has been demonstrated.

From the lemma, it follows that point OOO is the intersection point of the perpendicular bisector of segment BCBCBC and line AXAXAX, which is namely point XXX.
Now, considering a point A′A'A′ on ray OAOAOA such that OA×OA′=202OA \times OA' = 20^2OA×OA′=202, this point is a fixed point independent of BBB and CCC and is always on Ω\OmegaΩ by the power of a point theorem. Thus, the line we seek, denoted as ℓ\ellℓ, is the perpendicular bisector of segment AA′AA'AA′. Particularly, the distance from this to OOO is given by (21+202/21)/2=841/42(21 + 20^2 / 21) / 2 = 841 / 42(21+202/21)/2=841/42, so the sought value is 883\textbf{883}883.
Additionally, as an alternative solution, one can also consider the inversion with respect to Γ\GammaΓ. Due to the above fact, Ω\OmegaΩ is invariant under this inversion, meaning that the image A′A'A′ of point AAA (the same fixed point) is always on Ω\OmegaΩ.
