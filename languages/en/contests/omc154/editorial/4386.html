A downward rotation of the die is called AAA, and a rightward rotation is called BBB. The entire movement consists of a combination of 121212 of each, AAA and BBB. The earliest that 666 can be facing up is on the 222nd rotation. Additionally, it takes at least 444 rotations for 666 to point up again after it has first pointed upwards. Therefore, we can conclude that the maximum score is 666.
Now, after 666 is facing up, at least 111 must be facing up before 666 can point up again. Furthermore, if A/BA/BA/B is executed while 666 is pointing directly upwards, then the next time A/BA/BA/B is executed, and only then, 111 will be facing up. The same applies in reverse. Keeping these points in mind, let’s count the number of ways to achieve a score of 666. For a score of 666, there must be at least 111111 "blocks" of both AAAAAA and BBBBBB, meaning one can have 666 of one and 555 of the other (in the case of 12 "blocks", consider the first 11). Let's assume there are 666 of BBBBBB.
In this case, the number of ways to arrange 555 of AAAAAA and 666 of BBBBBB in a row is 11C5=462{}_{11}\mathrm{ C }_5=46211​C5​=462.
