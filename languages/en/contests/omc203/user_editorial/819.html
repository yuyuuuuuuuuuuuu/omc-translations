Rephrasing the conditions,

  • You can freely choose the stone on the leftmost end and the stone next to the green one on the right.
  • Once red, blue, yellow, and green are determined, the number of stones including those will be fixed at 4,3,2,14,3,2,14,3,2,1 respectively.

Thus, it can be understood that this corresponds to the number of ways to partition 999 into a sum of positive integers not exceeding 444.

Specific Examples

If 9=3+3+2+19=3+3+2+19=3+3+2+1, then from the leftmost end, it corresponds to (blue, yellow, green)(blue, yellow, green)(yellow, green)(green)
If 9=4+1+1+1+29=4+1+1+1+29=4+1+1+1+2, then from the leftmost end, it corresponds to (red, blue, yellow, green)(green)(green)(green)(yellow, green)

Next, you can investigate as follows:
The rearrangements of 9=4+4+19=4+4+19=4+4+1 are 333 ways
The rearrangements of 9=4+3+29=4+3+29=4+3+2 are 666 ways
The rearrangements of 9=4+3+1+19=4+3+1+19=4+3+1+1 are 121212 ways
The rearrangements of 9=3+3+39=3+3+39=3+3+3 are 111 way……
and so on.

However, this approach works well for sums of 444 numbers around 999, but it is thought to become quite difficult when it reaches around 111111.
