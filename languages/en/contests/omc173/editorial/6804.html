Since even numbers cannot be paired with each other, we can assign one odd number to each even number. Considering it modulo 666, we have:

  • 000 can be paired with 111 or 555
  • 222 can be paired with 333 or 555
  • 444 can be paired with 111 or 333

Therefore, when the modular 666 values of the pairs for 666 and 121212 match, the modular 666 values for the remaining even numbers are also determined, resulting in 2⋅8=162 \cdot 8 = 162⋅8=16 possibilities. Conversely, when they do not match, we find that there are 8×8=648 \times 8 = 648×8=64 possibilities, leading to a total of 80\mathbf{80}80.
