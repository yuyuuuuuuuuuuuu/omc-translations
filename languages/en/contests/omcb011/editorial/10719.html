A paper with a radius of 555 can always be seen, but other papers cannot be seen if they are under a larger paper. Therefore, the paper with a radius of 555 has two ways of being oriented, "front" and "back", while the other papers have three ways, "front", "back", and "invisible". When the visibility of each paper is determined, there exists an overlapping arrangement that realizes it. Thus, the total number of visible arrangements is 2×34=1622 \times 3^4 = \textbf{162}2×34=162.
