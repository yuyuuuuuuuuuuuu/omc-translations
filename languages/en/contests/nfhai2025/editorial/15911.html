Lemma 1 A convex polyhedron PPP is fantastical if and only if for every edge of TTT, there exists a vertex of PPP on that edge (including endpoints).

Proof (⇒)(\Rightarrow)(⇒) For each edge eee of TTT, let lll be the line extended from eee. Since lll and TTT have a common intersection, lll must also intersect with PPP. Moreover, since PPP is contained within the interior of TTT by the vertex condition, it follows that PPP and eee must also have a common intersection.

(⇐)(\Leftarrow)(⇐) Take a line lll parallel to some edge of TTT.

  • If lll is perpendicular to the base of TTT
    Select one vertex of PPP on each edge of TTT that is perpendicular to the base of TTT, and let the convex polyhedron formed by these vertices be P′P^{\prime}P′. By the convexity of PPP, it follows that PPP contains P′P^{\prime}P′ in its interior. Furthermore, projecting P′P^{\prime}P′ onto the plane containing the base of TTT results in the base of TTT, so when lll intersects with TTT, it must also intersect with P′P^{\prime}P′. Therefore, if lll and TTT have a common intersection, then lll and PPP must also have a common intersection.
  • If lll is parallel to the base of TTT
    There are four edges of TTT parallel to lll. Select one vertex of PPP on each of these edges to form a tetrahedron, denoted as P0P_0P0​. By the convexity of PPP, it follows that PPP contains P0P_0P0​ in its interior. Additionally, the shape obtained by projecting P0P_0P0​ onto a plane perpendicular to lll is congruent to that of TTT, so when lll intersects with TTT, it must also intersect with P0P_0P0​. Therefore, if lll and TTT have a common intersection, then lll and PPP must also have a common intersection.

Lemma 2 A fantastical polyhedron PPP is dreamy if and only if the following two conditions are satisfied.

  • For each edge of TTT, either one of the endpoints is a vertex of PPP or the midpoint of that edge is a vertex of PPP, but not both.
  • If a vertex vvv of TTT is a vertex of PPP, then there exists a vertex v′v^{\prime}v′ connected to vvv by an edge of TTT such that v′v^{\prime}v′ is not a vertex of PPP.

Proof (⇒)(\Rightarrow)(⇒) Suppose there is an edge ABABAB of TTT such that the midpoint MMM and one endpoint AAA are vertices of PPP. Then the other endpoint BBB cannot be a vertex of PPP. (⇐)(\Leftarrow)(⇐) Suppose there is a fantastical polyhedron QQQ contained in PPP. For any vertex AAA of PPP, if AAA is the midpoint of an edge of TTT, then by the first condition, the only vertex of PPP on that edge is AAA. In any case, we can find an edge of TTT containing AAA such that the only vertex of PPP on that edge is AAA, and by extending that edge to form a line lll, it follows that AAA must be contained in QQQ. Therefore, QQQ contains all vertices of PPP, so PPP must be equal to QQQ. Thus, we have shown the result.
