There is a horizontal table and one piece of colored paper shaped like a circle with radii of 1,2,3,4,51, 2, 3, 4, 51,2,3,4,5. For i=1,2,3,4,5i=1,2,3,4,5i=1,2,3,4,5, the surface of the colored paper with radius iii is painted color 2i−12i-12i−1, and the back side is painted color 2i2i2i.
Also, colors 1,2,…,101, 2, \ldots, 101,2,…,10 are assumed to be distinct.
A fixed point is determined on the table, and a total of five pieces of colored paper are stacked on top of each other, with the fixed point coinciding with all the centers. The stacking order and which side is facing up can be chosen freely. How many distinct views of the five pieces of colored paper are there when viewed from directly above?
Even if the stacking order is different or the orientations of the sides are different, if the view from directly above is the same, those stackings are not distinguished.
