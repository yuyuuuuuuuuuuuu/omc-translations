A pentacube that meets the following conditions is called a good pentacube:

  • Starting with 111 cube, the shape is obtained by repeatedly gluing cubes together so that each new cube shares a face with only 111 existing cube.

When gluing a cube so that it shares a face with only 111 cube, the surface area of the solid increases by 444, so the surface area of a good pentacube is 6+4⋅4=226 + 4 \cdot 4 = 226+4⋅4=22. Here, a pentacube not being a good pentacube is equivalent to the following:

  • It contains a shape formed by arranging 444 cubes in a square 2×2×12 \times 2 \times 12×2×1 configuration.
