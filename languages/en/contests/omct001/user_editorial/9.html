To show that the greatest common divisor of A,B,C,DA, B, C, DA,B,C,D is either 111 or a power of 222:

  • If all are multiples of 333, the sum of the 888 integers appearing in the digits of A,B,C,DA, B, C, DA,B,C,D will be a multiple of 333, but since the sum is 383838, 333 cannot be a common divisor.
  • Since at most one multiple of 555 can appear, 555 cannot be a common divisor.
  • If all are multiples of 777, the only numbers that can appear are 333, 555, and 666, which correspond to 353535, 565656, and 636363, respectively. In this case, there will always be duplicates, so 777 cannot be a common divisor.
  • Since all two-digit multiples of 111111 have equal digits in the tens and units places, 111111 cannot be a common divisor.
  • If each of 13,17,19,2313, 17, 19, 2313,17,19,23 is a common divisor, focusing on 4,2,2,54, 2, 2, 54,2,2,5 leads to contradictions.
  • For prime numbers ppp greater than or equal to 252525, there can be at most 333 multiples of ppp between 101010 and 100100100, so they cannot be common divisors.
