The movement of PPP is assigned the following names.

  • Operation XXX: Move point PPP by 111 unit in the xxx direction
  • Operation YYY: Move point PPP by 111 unit in the yyy direction
  • Operation ZZZ: Move point PPP by 111 unit in the zzz direction

We consider matching a string consisting of X,Y,ZX, Y, ZX,Y,Z to the ways of moving PPP. For example, the sequence of operations X→Y→Z→⋯X \rightarrow Y \rightarrow Z \rightarrow \cdotsX→Y→Z→⋯ corresponds to the string XYZ⋯XYZ\cdotsXYZ⋯. Now, for strings consisting of XXX and YYY, we will refer to selecting one of each of XXX and YYY and replacing them with ZZZ as operation QQQ. The following lemma holds.

Lemma. For all strings that can be formed by rearranging nnn XXXs and mmm YYYs, when operation QQQ is performed once at each possible location, the strings that can be formed by rearranging n−1n-1n−1 XXXs, m−1m-1m−1 YYYs, and 222 ZZZs will each appear exactly 222 times.

Proof. For the string TTT formed by rearranging n−1n-1n−1 XXXs, m−1m-1m−1 YYYs, and 222 ZZZs, the positions of XXX and YYY in TTT correspond to positions of XXX and YYY, and the positions of the two ZZZs will correspond to the two strings where X,YX, YX,Y or Y,XY, XY,X are located in order, hence the lemma follows.
