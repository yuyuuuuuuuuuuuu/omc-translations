In fact, BD=CDBD = CDBD=CD can be demonstrated in various ways. (The approach after showing BD=CDBD = CDBD=CD is the same as the official explanation.) Therefore, here we will introduce the general flow of two methods to show BD=CDBD = CDBD=CD. \\ Method 1

  • Let the circumcenter of triangle BECBECBEC be FFF. Then, triangle FECFECFEC becomes an equilateral triangle.
  • Since ∠FCD=∠ECD=30∘∠FCD = ∠ECD = 30^\circ∠FCD=∠ECD=30∘, it follows that triangles FCDFCDFCD and ECDECDECD are congruent, and we can conclude that ∠FDC=∠EDC=42∘∠FDC = ∠EDC = 42^\circ∠FDC=∠EDC=42∘.
  • From DF⊥BCDF \perp BCDF⊥BC and BF=CFBF = CFBF=CF, the line DFDFDF is the perpendicular bisector of BCBCBC, leading to BD=CDBD = CDBD=CD.

Method 2

  • Let the circumcenter of triangle DECDECDEC be LLL. Then, triangle DLEDLEDLE becomes an equilateral triangle, and through simple angle calculations, we find that ∠BCL=30∘∠BCL = 30^\circ∠BCL=30∘.
  • Let the intersection of line CLCLCL and line BEBEBE be KKK. Then, we have BK=CKBK = CKBK=CK and ∠LKE=60∘∠LKE = 60^\circ∠LKE=60∘.
  • Since ∠LKE=∠LDE=60∘∠LKE = ∠LDE = 60^\circ∠LKE=∠LDE=60∘, the four points K,D,E,LK, D, E, LK,D,E,L are concyclic, and by the inscribed angle theorem, we find that ∠KDL=∠KEL=24∘∠KDL = ∠KEL = 24^\circ∠KDL=∠KEL=24∘.
  • From ∠KDC=∠KDL+∠LDC=42∘∠KDC = ∠KDL + ∠LDC = 42^\circ∠KDC=∠KDL+∠LDC=42∘, it follows that DK⊥BCDK \perp BCDK⊥BC, and since BK=CKBK = CKBK=CK, the line DKDKDK is the perpendicular bisector of BCBCBC, leading to BD=CDBD = CDBD=CD.
