Considering the arrangement of coins in each square, where the coins in squares with pieces are face up and the coins in squares without pieces are face down, one operation corresponds to the following:

  • Flipping two adjacent coins.

In this case, removing all pieces from the squares is equivalent to flipping the coins in the squares where the pieces were initially placed an odd number of times, while the other coins are flipped an even number of times. Therefore, by performing one operation on each of the 8 pairs of adjacent coins along the shortest path from the square where the piece was initially placed to the other squares, the minimum number of operations can be achieved. Since the order of operations is arbitrary, the total number of ways to achieve this is
