It is sufficient to count the values of nnnn^{n^n}nnn that are not perfect squares. nnn needs to be odd, and in this case, nnn itself must also be a non-perfect square. Among the 555 odd numbers in the range from 1 to 1110, there are 17 perfect squares: 12,32,…,3321^2, 3^2, \ldots, 33^212,32,…,332. Therefore, the number we seek is 555−17=538555 - 17 = \mathbf{538}555−17=538.
