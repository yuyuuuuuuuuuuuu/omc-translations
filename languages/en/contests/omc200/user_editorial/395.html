In general, when we denote the kkk-th symmetric polynomial of the variables x1,⋯ ,xnx_1, \cdots, x_nx1​,⋯,xn​ as SkS_kSk​, it is known that the mmm-th power sum of x1,⋯ ,xnx_1, \cdots, x_nx1​,⋯,xn​ can be expressed as a polynomial with integer coefficients in terms of S1,⋯ ,SmS_1, \cdots, S_mS1​,⋯,Sm​. This can be demonstrated, for example, from Newton's identities.

Assuming this, the desired 444-th power sum can be expressed using only symmetric polynomials up to the 444-th degree. Considering the relationship between roots and coefficients, the coefficients of terms of degree 111 or lower can be set arbitrarily without changing the 444-th power sum of the roots. That is, the following two sums are equal.

  • The 444-th power sum of the (counting multiplicities) complex roots of 32x6−48x4+18x2−x−1=032x^6 - 48x^4 + 18x^2 - x - 1 = 032x6−48x4+18x2−x−1=0.
  • The 444-th power sum of the (counting multiplicities) complex roots of 32x6−48x4+18x2=032x^6 - 48x^4 + 18x^2 = 032x6−48x4+18x2=0.

Since the 444-th power sum does not change even if we do not include the root x=0x=0x=0, dividing the left-hand side of the latter equation by x2x^2x2 does not change it either. This fills in the gaps.
