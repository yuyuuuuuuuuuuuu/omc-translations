How many ways are there to place the 10 balls, each with a unique integer from 1 to 10, into one of the three distinguishable boxes A, B, or C such that the following two conditions are satisfied?

  • Each of the three boxes must contain at least one ball.
  • Let the products of the numbers on the balls placed in A, B, and C be denoted as a,b,ca, b, ca,b,c respectively. The greatest common divisor of these three numbers must be 1.
