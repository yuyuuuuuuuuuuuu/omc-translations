There is a grid of 13×1313\times 1313×13 squares and a normal cube-shaped die with a side length of 111. The die is initially placed with the face showing 111 facing up, exactly positioned at the top left square.
Now, we consider rolling (or tipping) this die along the edges to share squares repeatedly, moving to the bottom right square in the shortest possible number of moves. In this case, for a certain movement method, we define the score as follows:

  • The number of squares among the total of 252525 squares (including the starting and ending points) that the die has passed through where the face showing 666 is facing up.

Let the maximum possible score be MMM. How many different sets of 252525 squares can there be through which the die passes in a movement that results in a score of MMM?

Note that a normal cube-shaped die has one of the integers from 111 to 666 corresponding to each face, with the sum of the integers on opposite faces always being 777. Furthermore, the arrangement of the die faces and the initial placement may vary, but the answer to this problem does not depend on these variations.
