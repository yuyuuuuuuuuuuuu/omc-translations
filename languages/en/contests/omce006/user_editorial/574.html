Introduction
The circle that is tangent to the circumcircle of a triangle and also tangent to two sides of that triangle is called the Mixtilinear excircle, and it seems to have properties similar to the Mixtilinear incircle. In this explanation, I will refer to the one located inside ∠A\angle A∠A as the A-Mixtilinear excircle. According to the problem statement, the radius of the A-Mixtilinear excircle will be denoted as aaa.
Let the points where the incircle of triangle △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC intersects the sides BC,CA,ABBC, CA, ABBC,CA,AB be denoted as D,E,FD, E, FD,E,F, respectively, and we define

Geometry Part 2
Next, we will express a,b,ca, b, ca,b,c in terms of x,y,zx, y, zx,y,z. Since there is symmetry in the diagram, we will aim to find aaa here.
Similar to the official explanation, we consider the operation τττ that reflects through the line AIAIAI after inverting with respect to a circle centered at AAA with radius AB×AC\sqrt{AB\times AC}AB×AC​. The A-Mixtilinear excircle is tangent to the lines AB,ACAB, ACAB,AC, and also to the circumcircle ΓΓΓ of triangle △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC, so the image under the operation τττ will be a circle tangent to the lines AB,AC,BCAB, AC, BCAB,AC,BC, which is the incircle of triangle △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC. Conversely, performing the operation τττ on the incircle of triangle △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC leads us to the A-Mixtilinear excircle. Here is a fact:

Fact. When performing the operation τττ on a circle with center III and radius rrr (not passing through AAA), the image will be a circle with radius r×AB×AC∣r2−AI2∣\dfrac{r\times AB\times AC}{|r^2-AI^2|}∣r2−AI2∣r×AB×AC​.
Proof. Please refer to the explanation of OMC113-F. Note that in this case, the radius of the circle being inverted is not 111 but AB×AC\sqrt{AB\times AC}AB×AC​, so the radius of the image circle will be scaled by (AB×AC)2(\sqrt{AB\times AC})^2(AB×AC​)2.
