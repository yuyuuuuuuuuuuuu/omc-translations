We will call the operation of adding 1 Operation 1 and the operation of doubling Operation 2.

In this case, when considering binary, Operation 1 corresponds to changing the least significant bit from 0 to 1, and Operation 2 corresponds to adding a 0 to the left (the operation of performing Operation 1 twice can be replaced by first performing Operation 1 once and then performing Operation 2, so we do not need to consider the case when the least significant bit is 1).

Here, since performing Operation 1 does not increase the number of digits in binary, it is necessary to perform Operation 2 at least (number of digits in binary)−1(\text{number of digits in binary}) - 1(number of digits in binary)−1 times. Also, since performing Operation 2 does not increase the digit sum in binary, it is necessary to perform Operation 1 at least (digit sum in binary)−1(\text{digit sum in binary}) - 1(digit sum in binary)−1 times.
