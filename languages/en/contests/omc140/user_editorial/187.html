This is an alternative counting method for the explanation of the formula "the way of pointing for people who are not in pairs."

Considering the complementary event, it is only in the case where 222 out of the 333 people who are not in pairs form 111 pair. The number of ways to choose the 222 people to form a pair is 333, and the way for the remaining 111 person to point is 666, so there are 3×6=183 \times 6 = 183×6=18 ways. Therefore, the number of cases we are looking for is 63−18=1986^3 - 18 = 19863−18=198 ways.
