About Grundy Numbers Games that possess the following characteristics are referred to as impartial games, and this problem falls under this category.

  • Two players take turns to make moves.
  • One player wins, while the other loses.
  • The possible states are finite, and any move will eventually lead to a state where no further moves are possible.
  • Moves are not influenced by chance.
  • Both players have complete information about the game.
  • The moves available to both players are identical for the same state.
  • The player who cannot make a move loses.
