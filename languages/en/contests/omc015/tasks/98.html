There is a finite sequence composed of positive integers, XXX. We repeatedly perform the following operations on XXX.

  • First, select two adjacent numbers. However, the numbers at the ends must not be included.
  • If both selected adjacent numbers are 2 or greater, reduce each of the selected two numbers by 1 and insert two 1's between them.
  • If both selected adjacent numbers are 1, increase by 1 the two numbers adjacent to these and remove the selected two numbers.
  • If one of the selected adjacent numbers is 1, reduce the other number by 1, and increase the number adjacent to the 1 that is not selected.

For example, the operations proceed as follows:

{2,(3,7),8,1,4}→{2,2,(1,1),6,8,1,4}→{2,3,7,(8,1),4}→{2,3,7,7,1,5}\lbrace 2,(3,7),8,1,4\rbrace\rightarrow \lbrace 2,2,(1,1),6,8,1,4\rbrace\rightarrow \lbrace 2,3,7,(8,1),4\rbrace\rightarrow \lbrace 2,3,7,7,1,5\rbrace{2,(3,7),8,1,4}→{2,2,(1,1),6,8,1,4}→{2,3,7,(8,1),4}→{2,3,7,7,1,5}
The operation is terminated when the length of XXX becomes 3 or less. It can be proven that the operations will always terminate in a finite number of steps.
