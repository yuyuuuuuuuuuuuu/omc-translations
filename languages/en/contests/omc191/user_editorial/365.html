In diagrams involving the incenter, there is often a lot of angle information, which makes length calculations work out well.
You can think of length calculations as one of the geometric methods to obtain the desired expressions using area ratios, the law of sines, and trigonometric ratios.

Well-known fact
In triangle ABCABCABC, let the incenter be III, and let the points where the incircle intersects the sides BC,CA,ABBC, CA, ABBC,CA,AB be D,E,FD, E, FD,E,F, respectively. If we take the line segment DPDPDP to be the diameter of the incircle, and let RRR be the intersection of APAPAP and BCBCBC, the following holds: BR=CDBR=CDBR=CD
