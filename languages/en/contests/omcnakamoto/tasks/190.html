There is a board divided into squares of equilateral triangles, each with a side length of 1cm\displaystyle 1\text{cm}1cm, forming a regular hexagon with integer side lengths in cm\text{cm}cm. The first figure shows the case when the side length is 3cm\displaystyle 3\text{cm}3cm. Two squares are said to be facing each other if they share exactly one vertex, and one of the squares can overlap with the other by rotating it 180∘\displaystyle 180^{\circ}180∘ around the shared vertex. For example, the squares that are facing the black circle in the second figure are the three white circles.

Shami and Momo play a game using pieces and this board. In the game, the following operations are performed repeatedly:

  • First, Momo selects one unoccupied square on the board and places one pink piece in that square.
  • Next, Shami selects one unoccupied square that is facing the square where Momo just placed a piece and places one purple piece there.

This operation continues until one of them can no longer make a move, and the number of purple pieces placed on the board becomes Shami's points. Shami tries to maximize his points while Momo tries to minimize Shami's points. When both players act optimally, how many points will Shami get?
