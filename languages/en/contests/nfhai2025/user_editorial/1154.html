A solid contained within a cube that appears the same as the cube when viewed from any of the three perpendicular directions to its faces is called an imaginary cube. A convex one is referred to as a convex imaginary cube, and among convex imaginary cubes, the one with the smallest volume is called a minimal convex imaginary cube.

For example, the cube itself is an imaginary cube, but it is not minimal. The solid formed by connecting the midpoints of all the edges is a minimal imaginary cube.
