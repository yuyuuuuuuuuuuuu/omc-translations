OMC-kun was supposed to write down the sum of the integers from 111 to 100100100 as

1+2+3+⋯+99+1001+2+3+\cdots+99+1001+2+3+⋯+99+100
without omitting any steps, but in his haste, he forgot to write the +++ sign in exactly 333 places. When he calculated the result of the expression he actually wrote, it turned out to be 119801198011980. How many possible expressions could OMC-kun have actually written?
For example, if he only forgot the +++ sign between 101010 and 111111, it would be interpreted as adding 101110111011 instead of 101010 and 111111, and the result would be 604060406040.
