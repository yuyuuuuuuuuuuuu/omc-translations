Answer 1. It is a necessary and sufficient condition for 333 diagonals to intersect at points other than the endpoints, and not to intersect at exactly 111 point. Here, the selection method that satisfies only the former condition corresponds one-to-one to the way of choosing 666 vertices from the vertices of a regular octagon, which is 282828 ways. Furthermore, there are 999 points inside the regular octagon where 333 or more diagonals can intersect, and among them, only the center has 444 diagonals passing through. Therefore, the choices that satisfy the former but not the latter condition amount to 121212 ways. Hence, the total number of cases we are looking for is 16\textbf{16}16 ways.

Answer 2. When 333 diagonals divide the paper into 777 parts, there will always be a triangular region at the center. Conversely, in a regular octagon where all diagonals are drawn, the selection of 333 diagonals that meet the conditions can be restored from each small triangle that does not share vertices with the regular octagon. Therefore, it suffices to count the number of such small triangles, which is 16\textbf{16}16.
