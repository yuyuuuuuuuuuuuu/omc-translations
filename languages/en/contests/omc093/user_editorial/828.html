When BC=AD=72BC=AD=72BC=AD=72, there may be a question of whether an inscribed circle actually exists. The official explanation does not mention this at all, but such an inscribed circle indeed exists.
To express it in a more generalized way, it is shown that "if quadrilateral ABCDABCDABCD satisfies AB+CD=BC+ADAB+CD=BC+ADAB+CD=BC+AD, then an inscribed circle must exist."

This theorem is the converse of what is called "Pitot's Theorem," but surprisingly, it was proven only in the 19th century, which is relatively recent in the history of mathematics (the proof itself is not that difficult, though).
