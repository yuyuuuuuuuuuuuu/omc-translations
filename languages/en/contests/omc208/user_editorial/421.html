Even without the idea that different positions in a checkerboard pattern can be considered independently, it is possible to establish a recurrence relation as follows.

In a 2×n2 \times n2×n grid, the left 2×(n−1)2 \times (n-1)2×(n−1) squares satisfy the conditions, and we classify based on whether the two squares in the rightmost column satisfy the conditions.
Let ana_nan​ be the number of cases where both squares in the rightmost column satisfy the conditions.
Let bnb_nbn​ be the number of cases where one of the two squares in the rightmost column satisfies the conditions.
Let cnc_ncn​ be the number of cases where neither of the two squares in the rightmost column satisfies the conditions.
We denote the color of the rightmost column as follows: the first row is square 111 with color XXX, and the second row is square 222 with color YYY. Now, we consider adding a new column n+1n+1n+1 to the right and coloring it either red or blue.

  • When both squares 111 and 222 satisfy the conditions:
    We can paint the square to the right of square 111 the same color as XXX or a different color from XXX, and similarly for square 222 with respect to YYY. Each of these four cases contributes uniquely to an+1,bn+1,bn+1,cn+1a_{n+1}, b_{n+1}, b_{n+1}, c_{n+1}an+1​,bn+1​,bn+1​,cn+1​.
  • When either square 111 or square 222 satisfies the conditions:
    In both cases where only square 111 or only square 222 satisfies the conditions, we can treat the satisfying square as 111. The square to the right of square 111 must be painted a different color from YYY. Depending on whether we paint the square to the right of square 222 a different color from XXX or the same color as XXX, we contribute to an+1a_{n+1}an+1​ and bn+1b_{n+1}bn+1​.
  • When neither square 111 nor square 222 satisfies the conditions:
    We need to paint the squares to the right of squares 111 and 222 in such a way that they satisfy the conditions, and this painting method is unique. Furthermore, when this unique method is chosen, the squares to the right of squares 111 and 222 will be different colors, contributing entirely to an+1a_{n+1}an+1​.
