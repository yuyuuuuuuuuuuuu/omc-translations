There are cards with integers written on them from 111 to 888, one for each integer. These cards are distributed to players AAA and BBB in groups of 444 each, and a game is played as follows:

  • Player AAA goes first, and Player BBB goes second, alternately playing one card from their distributed cards. A card cannot be played again once it has been played.
  • A player can only play a card with an integer that is coprime to the integer written on the card just played by their opponent. The game ends when a player cannot play a card following this rule.
  • The last player to play a card wins the game (specifically, if all cards are played, Player BBB wins).

Thus, if both players play optimally, Player AAA will win regardless of the situation. At this time, please calculate the total sum of all possible four-digit integers that can be formed by arranging the 444 cards distributed to Player AAA in descending order.
It is assumed that the cards held by both players are always revealed to each other.

Answer Format

For example, if Player AAA has the cards 1,2,3,41, 2, 3, 41,2,3,4, then the four-digit integer NNN that can be formed by arranging the four cards in descending order is N=4321N = 4321N=4321. Please calculate the total sum of such NNN for all distribution methods where Player AAA has a guaranteed win.
