On a number line representing integers from 111 to 161616, perform the following operation any number of times (at least 111) to place one or more coins:

  • Select one integer kkk between 111 and 161616 that satisfies at least one of the following two conditions and place one coin at the point representing kkk. However, if no such kkk exists, the operation stops at that point (you may also stop the operation if such a kkk exists).
    • For all integers k′k^{\prime}k′ that satisfy 1≤k′≤k1 \leq k^{\prime} \leq k1≤k′≤k, no coins have been placed at the points representing k′k^{\prime}k′.
    • For all integers k′k^{\prime}k′ that satisfy k≤k′≤16k \leq k^{\prime} \leq 16k≤k′≤16, no coins have been placed at the points representing k′k^{\prime}k′.

How many ways can the coins be placed in this case? Note that even if the final positions of the coins are the same, different orders of placement are considered distinct.
